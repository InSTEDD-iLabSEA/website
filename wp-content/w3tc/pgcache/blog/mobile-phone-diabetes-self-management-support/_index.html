Mobile Phone Diabetes Self-Management Support

January 23, 2014

In Cambodia, access to reliable and useful information remains a challenge facing most Cambodians with diabetes. MoPoTsyo, a local NGO that focuses on diabetes, will use InSTEDD iLab SEA’s Verboice platform to provide patients tips and advices through interactive voice calls.

Verboice is an interactive voice response (IVR) technology that lets you create and run applications via voice, thereby overcoming literacy and geographical barriers.

Within the next twelve months from this month, a selected 250 diabetes patients will receive pre-recorded voice messages of reminders to appointments, take medication, make healthy food choices, and exercise regularly.

Heang Hen, a Monitoring Officer at MoPoTsyo Patient Information Center, says that with this Verboice, it’s “useful and faster to send educational voice messages and information to patients.”

Prior to using Verboice, MoPoTsyo started this mobile phone diabetes self-management support by sending text messages to its randomly-selected 500 patients. However, patient recipients encountered a number of challenges, including navigating to the SMS inbox, limited Khmer-language support on mobile phone, and language barrier.

MoPoTsyo’s mission is to improve access to reliable information for Cambodian diabetics, especially those who are poor. This project is part of BRIDGES (Bringing Research in Diabetes to Global Environments and Systems), a programme initiated by the International Diabetes Federation, and supported by an educational grant from Lilly Diabetes.

In September 2012, with funding from Spider (The Swedish Program for ICT in Developing Regions), InSTEDD iLab SEA introduced and extended Verboice, an open-source tool which specializes in using IVR to support health, safety and sustainable development work.
